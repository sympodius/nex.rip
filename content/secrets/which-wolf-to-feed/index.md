As I got closer to making and releasing music again, I wanted to talk about it with my friends.

The first person I really opened up to was Fabienne Black. I'm sure you know her name, both for her critically acclaimed solo work, and her wildly successful band, *Le Chien*. Fabienne came to fame around the same time as me, but she managed to maintain her success. She says the difference between us is that she came to fame slowly, while we were thrust into it. She had the time to develop good business relationships and learn the industry, while we had to bluff everything and put faith in people we barely knew.

In any case, Fabienne and I met in the cafeteria of a music-based television station in Sweden. We were both promoting our music, and we quickly found common ground (both being up-and-coming musicians from the same part of Scotland). However, what really got us chatting was talking about magic tricks. I don't remember how the topic came up, but we spent the better part of an hour performing illusions for each other before I got called to a green room. We've been great friends ever since.

I wanted Fabienne's opinion because she really saw my full rise and fall, but also because she's still in the music industry today. After speaking to her briefly on the phone, she invited me over to her house to talk it out in more detail. She's always been very generous that way.

My brief stint with success has afforded me a modestly comfortable lifestyle, without needing to worry too much about money. However, Fabienne's continued success has allowed her to be... very comfortable. Having said that, she's not a very 'bling' person. Her home is a restored Victorian-era farm in the suburban pastures of Glasgow. It consists of a fairly luxurious six-bedroom farmhouse, a lot of land, and a converted barn that now functions as her home studio and rehearsal space. Nothing in the farmhouse looks new though. Fabienne prefers to fill her life with well-made things that last. Some of them she bought new and took good care of, others she got someone else to restore for her.

I couldn't say the same for her studio. Everything in there looks like it's been beaten up and thrashed to within an inch of its life. The creative spirit versus the calming soul, I suppose.

When I got to her place, Fabienne took me straight down to the studio. That signalled to me that she was taking this very seriously and wasn't expecting a relaxed chat.

I told her what I was thinking and asked if she thought it was a good idea, and if going the self-publishing route made sense for me.

Fabienne asked me a lot of follow up questions before answering. I'll try to capture what I remember:

"How important is it that people actually hear this music? I mean... is it more important that you just get it out of your head?"

"If I'm being honest, getting it out of my head is more important. But if it could maybe help people, that would be great."

"Are you wanting to work creatively with others, or is this more of a self-reflective solo thing?"

"I guess more solo. I wouldn't be opposed to working with other people but... I guess, since I'd not even thought about that, it's probably not a priority for me."

"And... you're okay for money?"

"Oh yeah, I mean... more or less. I think I'll maybe run out of money just about the time I die, but my pension should see me through that."

"Dark. Yeah, I think you should probably avoid the record labels and go it on your own. You'll be able to take things at your own pace and not have to worry about business stuff or appeasing anyone. You can just be all about the art. I think that's how you're going to be most fulfilled."

"You think it's worth doing though, Fab? It's not a stupid idea?"

"Oh, hell no, dude! Art is always good. You've got the talent and you've got the feelings: you gotta get them down. I've been hoping to hear more Nex tunes for years! What is it you want to write about anyway?"

"I'm not completely sure. I guess I just... I think the world is a beautiful place. There's so much goodness in it. But there always seem to be forces trying to take advantage of that: pitting us against each other and twisting our love toward hate. And it's always rooted in fear. When I see someone seize power, or treat others badly, or lack compassion... I don't see a strong person: I see a scared little child, desperately unwilling to accept that the world might have loved them for who they are, and instead striking first to avoid rejection. All they ever seem to do is cut themselves further off from real human connections, because they fear no one will like them for who they are. And the longer they act that way, the more right they become. Worse, they manipulate us by making us fear to lose the things we love. Fear is a self-fulfilling prophecy, Fab. When we let it into our hearts, it consumes us and makes that which we fear become true."

"Whoa, dude! Did you just come up with that? You got a little biblical at the end there."

"Haha! Nah, I guess it's been kicking about my head for a while."

"It sounds to me like you've got something you really want to say, dude. I think you gotta say it."

"But what if I make things worse? What if I can't get my point across, or people misinterpret it? Or, even worse, they understand me perfectly but find it so unconvincing that they decide to go the opposite way?"

"Ah, come on, Nex. If we all thought that way then nothing good would ever happen."

Fabienne has always been super supportive of my work. It was only at the end of our conversation that I realised she had never even questioned that I should be writing and releasing songs again; she was just considering the best way.

It's nice to have that sort of positivity---especially from someone I admire as much Fabienne---but I thought perhaps I needed a more... tempered opinion as well.

As such, I thought I should ask someone who has known me since I was a kid, has no ties to the music industry, and is something of a realist. I often like to call myself 'an optimistic realist,' but the guy I was going to chat with is a 'logistical realist.' He's not quite a pessimist... but he approaches everything so mechanically that it often feels that way.

Anyway, his name is Neil Crow. I knew Fabienne would be cool with me talking about her on this site, but I had to get permission from Neil. Not that he'd ever refuse it---he just doesn't like things being taken for granted. So, before I get into this, just know that I asked Neil if I could talk about him on this site, he said it was totally okay, and he told me that he trusted me to represent him accurately.

He said that last bit a little pointedly.

Neil still lives in the house he grew up in. He never moved out. Eventually his parents moved to Florida and he just bought the house from them. It's a nice house though. I grew up around the corner on the adjoining street. His family wasn't dripping with wealth or anything, but Neil definitely had the biggest place of all my friends growing up.

When I got to his place, he invited me in and put me in the living room. He showed up a minute later with tea and biscuits. He told me to help myself, then sat down in the armchair opposite me.

"It's good to see you, Nex. What brings you to the rough side of town?"

"The town might be rough, but you know damn well that we grew up on the nice side of it."

Neil chuckled and munched on a biscuit. I took a deep breath and told him my plans.

"Look Neil, I kind of want to do something, and it might mean giving up a little of my anonymity."

"But you love your anonymity. You told me it was a secure blanket that protected you from recrimination and judgement and allowed you to live a placid life of mild mannered contentment."

"Yeah, well: I might not be quite as content as I thought I was."

"Alright, tell me what you're planning then."

"I want to write music again and release it. I want to share my thoughts on the world and where we're going as a species."

"Uh huh. I mean... I suppose you could do that. You still make money from royalties, right?"

"Yeah? It gets less and less each year, but yeah."

"And I assume that you don't want to, you know... get a real job at any point?"

"Well... not as such, no."

"But the royalties will keep you alive till old age, right? You don't have to worry about money?"

"My accountant projected I should be okay."

"And what if people suddenly decided that they hate you?"

"Huh?"

"If people suddenly decided that they hate you, and they stripped out your music from all those films and TV shows, would you be able to survive?"

"I... I'm not sure. I suppose probably not. Or, at least. I'd have to live more simply or get a job or something."

Neil ate an entire biscuit over multiple bites and drank some tea before saying anything else.

"Sounds needlessly risky to me, man. You've got a pretty good setup: you get to enjoy yourself, keep out of the public eye, live a mild mannered life, and not really worry about... well, anything really."

I won't go into the rest of the conversation. I told him about my artistic motivations and about not feeling fulfilled, but he kept coming back to the risk of losing my 'sweet situation.'

It was a pretty big u-turn from the conversation with Fabienne, but they both have one thing in common: each has my best interest at heart.

Fabienne wants me to be emotionally fulfilled and excited about my life; Neil wants me to be safe and content. Neither of them are wrong in their outlook. Talking to them both was valuable. They both made me think about things I'd not really considered. Fabienne made me realise that it's obvious I'll be happier creating things, even if I had to deal with the crap I went through before. Neil made me realise that messing with the status quo might have unexpected consequences that I'm not going to be happy about.

But even those consequences don't sound that bad to me. I live a charmed life right now. So what if I lost it all and died in the street? I'd still have had a better life than most people. I've never taken it for granted, but most people never get the kind of breaks I did. I could probably die today and be happy with how my life went. Yet, the idea of continuing this way doesn't sound great to me.

Here's the thing: I told Fabienne that fear is the source of most of the negative forces in the world. That's just my opinion. But I also think it keeps us safe sometimes. I don't mind being afraid. I think fear is as important to humanity as love. I just don't want it to be the driving force in my life.

This whole thing has reminded me of that story about the wolves. It goes like this: there are two wolves inside of us and they are always fighting. One wolf is darkness and fear; the other is light and hope. Which wolf wins?

Whichever one you feed.

I've got a whole bag of kibble sitting here, and I know who I'm feeding.
