So... hi, I guess.

It's been a long time... or... well... if you remember me then it's been a long time.

But then why would you be here if you didn't remember me? I suppose you could just be here by accident. Oh! Or maybe you're from the future and you've read a newer post, and now you've come back to find out where it all started! That would be cool.

Okay, John's looking over my shoulder and says this isn't good content. He says I need to tell you what the site's about...

Right, well, the site is about... no, I better tell you who I am first in case you don't know.

I'm **Nex** (or *Nexami Engeo*, to my lawyers), and I was---briefly---famous. When I was a teenager, I was in a band called *[engeo](https://engeo.bandcamp.com)*[^engeo] with my brother, Jaime. We'd been making EPs in our bedroom, and we were starting to put together our first album when one of our songs took off. Then things went wild! We were doing all this press stuff; making crazy bank; going on TV shows; mixing with famous folk... man, it was a trip.

Getting traction on that song got us our record deal. We got thrown into a studio to get our first album recorded 'properly,' but it felt like we lost control of the music at that point. Definitely a million miles away from recording in our bedroom.

The album came out and it did really well. I mean, it wasn't number one or anything, but it was in the top ten in loads of places. Critical acclaim and all that stuff. We were touring for a while. People were always really nice to us: showing up to gigs and telling us how much they liked the music. It was great. Really life-affirming stuff.

But then there was all this pressure to get the next album out, and we had to write most of it on the tour bus. I dunno, it all just felt kinda... forced? We'd not had a minute to catch our breath before we were back in the studio recording album number two.

The second album didn't do so well. Which, you know... fair enough. It wasn't very good. If we'd taken more notice of the fine print in our contract, we might have realised that being legally required to get a second album out within a year might not be the most conducive atmosphere for creativity.

Freakin' suits, man. The minute the response wasn't blindingly positive, they just ghosted us. We couldn't get anything else done with them, and they wouldn't let us move on to a different label. They owned both albums and the single that got us noticed in the first place, so they just squeezed us dry.

I can't complain too much. I mean, we made tons of money and got to be rock stars for a couple of years. Not a lot of people get to do that. But since then I've just been living my life quietly and not doing too much of anything. I've got a nice flat in Glasgow. All the old recording equipment Jaime and I used is in a little room at the end of the hall. Sorry, that sounds forlorn. It's really been a lovely couple of decades!

I mean, for me it has. The world's been getting pretty weird. It's brought up a lot of feelings, and I wanted to get them out of my head. I found myself playing with the old equipment and knocking out a couple of tunes. It felt really cathartic.

They're probably not super insightful or anything, but I thought maybe the songs might comfort someone. The old songs seemed to reach people back in the day. I'm not under contract to the record company anymore, so I could sign up with someone else to release the songs. I don't think I want to go through all that again though. The only *engeo* music that's even available anymore is those first two EPs. The record company still owns the albums and they pulled them out of circulation. Which also means my royalties have dried up.

I was thinking back to when we made that early music though. Man, Jaime and I had a good time: just letting the music flow out of us. It was all so much more... well... fun. So, I'm going to ignore the record companies, and stay out of proper studios. I want to get back to that stream of pure creativity, where we had control over everything, and no one else decided what we should sound like or what we should play.

So, yeah. That's what this site is going to be. I'm going to make some tunes here, in my little room. I'm going to release them on this site. And I'm going to document everything. I'm going to document the tools, the theory, the journey... and anything that impacts me along the way. I'm really hoping this site will be the sort of thing that Jaime and I wished we'd had back in the day. We never really knew what we were doing. It was all trial and error, and we got so much wrong. But, you know, considering the equipment we had and how little we knew, those first couple of EPs sounded pretty freakin' rock'n'roll.

I'm expecting this site will mostly be demos, stems, tutorials, reviews, stuff going on in my life... and maybe I'll even manage a finished album or something down the line. But the point is that you can take the journey with me... if you want. All the secrets exposed. No hidden knowledge. No gatekeepers. No freakin' suits telling us how it should be.

I've roped in John to help out too (that's *John Urquhart Ferguson*, if you don't know). He helped us make our old website back in the day, so I got him in to do this one. He has his own blog at [haiverin.scot](https://haiverin.scot), and I thought it looked pretty cool, so I just asked him to make me the same thing---but like a comic book in the future. I think he did pretty well. He's also going to help with some of the technical stuff for recording here in the flat. He's been a bit of a mother hen, which is sort of annoying, but he knows his stuff.

I'm a little worried he might try to edit some of these posts, though. He told me he was just going to add 'mark-up'(?) to stuff. I don't know what that means, but it sounds like editing to me. You reading this, John? I don't want you editing my words, okay? The whole point of this is the artist has full control, alright?

I guess that's everything. At the moment it feels like Nex is dead and forgotten. RIP. Long buried. But I'm doing my best to bring him back. Maybe we'll all learn a little something along the way? Hopefully it helps you folks that are just starting out.

The battle-cry here at *Nex (RIP)* is this: **NEX LIVES!**

[^engeo]: Back in the day we told people our family name wasn't really *engeo*. We said it was just the phonetic spelling of *NGO* (*Non-Governmental Organisation*). We did enjoy our little word games.
