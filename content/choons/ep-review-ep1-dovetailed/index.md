I'm always interested in collaborations between respected artists, so when I heard that Kim Grant from [Raveloe](https://www.raveloe.com/) and Simon Liddell from [Olympic Swimmers](https://olympicswimmers.bandcamp.com/) and [Frightened Rabbit](https://frightenedrabbit.com/) were producing music together, I had to investigate.

Their first release, *[EP1](https://lostmap.bandcamp.com/album/ep1)*, is a lure. It's a bait to tease us so that we want more; a small sampling to get us craving the full meal. And it's working.

The release is sold exclusively on a limited edition cassette (with download code included). I don't know whether that's to create demand or to limit exposure while they find their sound, but either way I'm here for it.

Dovetailed's sound is mellow and gentle. It's grateful, but with a hint of longing. It's deeply atmospheric, but somehow welcoming. It puts me in mind of climbing a hill in a forest, where you can see the trail ahead is steeped in fog, but you still wish to go on. And once the world has disappeared and all you can see is a tree or two ahead, and a bed of grass below you, the problems of the world seem to melt away. Even if just for a moment, you can lie down and let the music wash over you, giving you respite and peace.

And yet, the lyrics in *Clearing* push you to take part in the world and not let it pass you by. While the comforting sounds bring energy, the lyrics encourage you to spend it wisely.

*Kethers Street* is a more energising number, celebrating personal growth and fulfilment. *Kitchen,* conversely, speaks of the benefits of patience and revelling in the unknown. For it is in the unknown that all possibilities lie, and we can hope for the best of them. This dreamy pairing in the middle of the EP makes me think there is a planned resilience in the way Kim and Simon approach their lives, and I think we could all benefit from a similar outlook.

A tender acoustic guitar riff draws us into *Secret Deaths.* I feel this song is about embracing dreams, or that's what it said to me. Or, perhaps, being receptive to the possibilities of dreams when they present themselves.

I cannot know the mind of Dovetailed any more than I can know yours, but listening to this EP makes me feel temporarily relieved from the world. It allows me to turn off my mind for a moment, letting the muscles relax and recover and cool off. A warm mug of hot chocolate for the weary thoughts, rejuvenating me to push on.

This indie-folk release has been recorded with soft intimacy, but far-reaching arrangement. It's an interplay between, "You've done so well: take a break," and, "You can do this: keep going; I believe in you!" For these days when the world can feel so trying, it's a refreshing experience to journey through *EP1*.

[EP1 is available now](https://lostmap.bandcamp.com/album/ep1).
